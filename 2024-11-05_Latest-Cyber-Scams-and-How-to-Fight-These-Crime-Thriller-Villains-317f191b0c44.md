### Chapter 1: Meet the Villains --- A Look at the Latest Cyber Scams {#2bb4 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="2bb4"}

- [**1. Phishing 2.0**: Explain how phishing has evolved. Highlight
  recent tactics, like spear-phishing (targeted), vishing (voice
  phishing), and smishing (SMS phishing).]{#0f66}
- [*Case Study*: Describe a recent phishing scam and how the attacker
  targeted the victim through an email that looked like it came from a
  trusted organization.]{#d104}
- [**2. Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)**: Explain the rise of ransomware
  being offered as a service, lowering the barrier to entry for
  criminals.]{#59fe}
- [*Case Study*: Mention a major ransomware attack (e.g., Colonial
  Pipeline incident) and how it crippled critical
  infrastructure.]{#a9bc}
- [**3. Business Email Compromise (BEC)**: Describe how hackers
  infiltrate or spoof corporate emails to steal funds.]{#f6b8}
- [*Case Study*: Share a story where a company transferred large sums
  due to a spoofed executive email.]{#47bb}
- [**4. Cryptojacking**: Explain how hackers are hijacking devices to
  mine cryptocurrency.]{#d535}
- [*Case Study*: Describe a cryptojacking incident on a popular website,
  explaining how users' devices were hijacked without their
  knowledge.]{#0aa1}
- [**5. Investment & Romance Scams**: Highlight how scammers lure
  victims with promises of high returns or emotional
  manipulation.]{#80ac}
- [*Case Study*: Share a story of a romance scam where the victim
  transferred funds, only to realize they were being deceived.]{#54ff}
- [**6. Fake Shopping Websites & E-commerce Fraud**: Describe how
  scammers use fake online stores and marketplaces to steal from
  unsuspecting shoppers.]{#42f5}
- [*Case Study*: Walk through an example of a fake online store,
  explaining how users lost money without receiving their goods.]{#a9bd}
