### Best CTF Platforms by Vijay Gupta {#c3fe .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading .graf--title name="c3fe"}

In the dynamic world of cybersecurity, Capture The Flag (CTF)
competitions serve as invaluable training grounds for ethical hackers to
sharpen their skills, solve challenges, and learn new techniques. These
platforms provide hands-on experience in real-world scenarios, where
participants must uncover vulnerabilities, exploit systems, and defend
against attacks. In this comprehensive guide curated by cybersecurity
enthusiast Vijay Gupta, we'll explore the top CTF platforms that offer
diverse challenges and opportunities for aspiring and seasoned ethical
hackers alike.

### 1. picoCTF: {#514d .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="514d"}

[picoCTF](https://picoctf.org/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://picoctf.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a
beginner-friendly CTF platform designed for students and beginners to
learn cybersecurity concepts through interactive challenges. Hosted by
Carnegie Mellon University, picoCTF offers a wide range of challenges
covering various categories, including cryptography, web exploitation,
reverse engineering, and forensics.

### 2. Ctftime: {#07fc .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="07fc"}

[Ctftime](https://ctftime.org/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://ctftime.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a
platform that aggregates information about upcoming CTF events, past
competitions, and team rankings. It serves as a centralized hub for the
CTF community, providing valuable insights into upcoming challenges and
opportunities for participants to compete and collaborate.

### 3. Parrot CTFs: {#6c61 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6c61"}

[Parrot CTFs](https://parrot-ctfs.com/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://parrot-ctfs.com/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is a platform designed for learning ethical hacking and
cybersecurity through interactive Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges. It
offers various scenarios that mimic real-world security vulnerabilities,
allowing users to practice and improve their skills in a controlled
environment.

### 4. Hack The Box: {#c4b8 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="c4b8"}

[Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.eu/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.hackthebox.eu/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is a popular platform that offers realistic virtual
labs and challenges for cybersecurity enthusiasts. With a focus on
hands-on learning, Hack The Box provides a wide range of machines and
challenges covering various topics, including penetration testing,
reverse engineering, and cryptography.

### 5. RootTheBox: {#404a .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="404a"}

[RootTheBox](https://www.rootthebox.com/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.rootthebox.com/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is a web-based CTF platform that allows users to create
and customize their own CTF competitions. It provides a user-friendly
interface for organizing challenges, managing teams, and monitoring
progress. RootTheBox is ideal for hosting private CTF events for
training or educational purposes.

### 6. TryHackMe: {#7308 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="7308"}

[TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://tryhackme.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is an
online platform that offers immersive cybersecurity labs and challenges
for beginners and professionals alike. With guided pathways and hands-on
exercises, TryHackMe provides a structured learning environment for
individuals looking to develop their cybersecurity skills.

### 7. OverTheWire: {#2c63 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="2c63"}

[OverTheWire](https://overthewire.org/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://overthewire.org/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} hosts a series of wargames that challenge players to
progressively solve levels by exploiting vulnerabilities and applying
creative problem-solving skills. From basic shell exploitation to
advanced cryptography challenges, OverTheWire offers a diverse range of
challenges suitable for all skill levels.

### 8. RingZer0 Team CTF: {#9193 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="9193"}

[RingZer0 Team CTF](https://ringzer0ctf.com/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://ringzer0ctf.com/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is a platform that hosts regular CTF challenges
covering various domains, including web security, cryptography, and
reverse engineering. With a focus on practical challenges and real-world
scenarios, RingZer0 Team CTF provides an engaging platform for honing
cybersecurity skills.

### 9. CTF365: {#96c0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="96c0"}

[CTF365](https://ctf365.com/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://ctf365.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a
cybersecurity training platform that simulates real-world cyber attacks
and defense scenarios. It offers a gamified environment where
participants can compete individually or in teams to secure virtual
networks, identify vulnerabilities, and defend against simulated
attacks.

### 10. VulnHub: {#cb6b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="cb6b"}

[VulnHub](https://www.vulnhub.com/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://www.vulnhub.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is
a repository of vulnerable virtual machines designed for practicing
penetration testing and ethical hacking skills. It offers a wide range
of intentionally vulnerable machines that users can download and deploy
in their virtualized environments to practice their skills.

### 11. HackThisSite: {#e225 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="e225"}

[HackThisSite](https://www.hackthissite.org/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.hackthissite.org/"
rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a community-driven platform that
offers a variety of hacking challenges, tutorials, and resources for
aspiring ethical hackers. From basic web challenges to advanced
cryptographic puzzles, HackThisSite provides a platform for learning and
experimentation.

### 12. Enigma Group: {#c493 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="c493"}

[Enigma Group](https://www.enigmagroup.org/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.enigmagroup.org/"
rel="noopener" target="_blank"} hosts a collection of web-based
challenges and tutorials covering topics such as SQL injection,
cross-site scripting (XSS), and network reconnaissance. It offers a
structured learning path for individuals looking to improve their web
security skills.

### 13. bWAPP: {#6f35 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6f35"}

[bWAPP (Buggy Web
Application)](http://www.itsecgames.com/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="http://www.itsecgames.com/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is an intentionally vulnerable web application that
provides a platform for practicing web application security testing
techniques. With various known vulnerabilities embedded in the
application, bWAPP allows users to learn and test their skills in a safe
environment.

### 14. Cryptopals: {#6494 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6494"}

[Cryptopals](https://cryptopals.com/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://cryptopals.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a
set of cryptographic challenges designed to teach participants about
common cryptographic vulnerabilities and attacks. From basic encryption
and decryption challenges to advanced cryptanalysis techniques,
Cryptopals offers a comprehensive learning path for mastering
cryptography.

### 15. CyberDefenders: {#5124 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="5124"}

[CyberDefenders](https://cyberdefenders.org/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://cyberdefenders.org/" rel="noopener"
target="_blank"} is a platform that offers hands-on cybersecurity
training and challenges for individuals looking to develop their skills
in offensive and defensive security. With guided exercises and practical
labs, CyberDefenders provides a platform for continuous learning and
skill development.

### 16. Hacksplaining: {#7b41 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="7b41"}

[Hacksplaining](https://www.hacksplaining.com/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.hacksplaining.com/"
rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is an educational platform that offers
interactive lessons and tutorials on various cybersecurity topics,
including web security, cryptography, and social engineering. With
engaging content and real-world examples, Hacksplaining provides a
user-friendly learning experience for individuals of all skill levels.

### 17. Hashcat: {#fe54 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="fe54"}

Hashcat is a powerful password recovery tool used for cracking passwords
through various methods, including brute-force attacks, dictionary
attacks, and rule-based attacks. It supports a wide range of hashing
algorithms and is highly efficient in cracking passwords stored in
hashed formats, such as MD5, SHA-1, and bcrypt.

### 18. Hellbound Hackers: {#b50f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b50f"}

[Hellbound Hackers](https://www.hellboundhackers.org/){.markup--anchor
.markup--p-anchor data-href="https://www.hellboundhackers.org/"
rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is an online community and resource
center for ethical hackers and security enthusiasts. It offers a variety
of challenges, tutorials, and forums covering topics such as hacking
techniques, programming languages, and cybersecurity news. Hellbound
Hackers aims to provide a supportive environment for individuals to
learn, share knowledge, and collaborate on cybersecurity projects.

### 19. Malware Hunting Tool: {#c33b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="c33b"}

The "Malware Hunting Tool" is a generic term that refers to software
tools used by cybersecurity professionals to detect, analyze, and remove
malware from infected systems. These tools may include antivirus
software, malware scanners, forensic analysis tools, and network traffic
analysis tools. They are essential for identifying and mitigating
malware threats in both individual systems and enterprise networks.

### 20. Mellivora: {#16a6 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="16a6"}

Mellivora is an intentionally vulnerable web application designed for
practicing web security testing techniques, such as SQL injection,
cross-site scripting (XSS), and command injection. It provides a
platform for cybersecurity enthusiasts to test their skills in a
controlled environment and learn about common web vulnerabilities and
exploitation techniques.

### 21. Nightshade: {#be5b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="be5b"}

Nightshade is an online platform that offers a variety of cybersecurity
challenges and competitions for individuals and teams. It covers a wide
range of topics, including cryptography, reverse engineering, web
exploitation, and network security. Nightshade provides an interactive
and engaging environment for participants to hone their skills and
compete against peers in real-world scenarios.

### 22. W3challs: {#012e .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="012e"}

[W3challs](https://w3challs.com/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://w3challs.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"} is a
web-based platform that offers a collection of hacking challenges and
CTF competitions for cybersecurity enthusiasts. It provides challenges
covering various categories, such as cryptography, web exploitation,
binary exploitation, and forensics. W3challs aims to foster learning and
collaboration among participants by providing a platform for practicing
cybersecurity skills in a gamified environment.

### 23. WebGoat: {#c1c0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="c1c0"}

WebGoat is an educational tool developed by OWASP (Open Web Application
Security Project) for teaching web application security concepts. It
provides a deliberately vulnerable web application that users can
interact with to learn about common web vulnerabilities, such as SQL
injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and insecure authentication
mechanisms. WebGoat is widely used by cybersecurity professionals and
students to gain practical experience in web security testing and
vulnerability assessment.

### Conclusion: {#a02c .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="a02c"}

These top CTF platforms offer diverse challenges and opportunities for
ethical hackers to enhance their skills, gain hands-on experience, and
contribute to the cybersecurity community. Whether you're a beginner
looking to get started in cybersecurity or a seasoned professional
seeking to expand your knowledge, these platforms provide invaluable
resources for learning and growth. So, dive in, explore, and embark on
your journey to becoming a proficient ethical hacker. Happy hacking!
