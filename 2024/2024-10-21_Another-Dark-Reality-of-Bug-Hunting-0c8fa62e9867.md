### Another Dark Reality of Bug Hunting {#253d .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading .graf--title name="253d"}

Bug hunting is one of the most rewarding careers in cybersecurity. It
promises the thrill of chasing down vulnerabilities, the satisfaction of
solving complex puzzles, and the excitement of contributing to global
cybersecurity. The field has grown significantly, with platforms like
HackerOne, Bugcrowd, and Synack offering hackers a legitimate way to
earn through discovering security loopholes. However, beyond the hype
and success stories, there exists another side to bug hunting --- a
darker, often less talked-about reality that can challenge even the most
determined bug hunters.

This blog will explore the less glamorous aspects of bug hunting, the
psychological strain, the uncertainty, the intense competition, and the
sometimes-unfair practices that bug hunters face.

### 1. The Emotional Roller Coaster {#8c32 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="8c32"}

Bug hunting is an emotional journey. While many associate bug bounty
hunting with triumphant moments of discovering vulnerabilities and
winning big bounties, the truth is that these highs are often followed
by deep lows. The process of identifying bugs, submitting reports, and
waiting for validation can stretch out for weeks or months.

### A. Constant Rejection {#36e7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="36e7"}

For every bug that gets rewarded, there are many that go unrecognized or
are marked as duplicates. Imagine spending hours or even days hunting
for a vulnerability, meticulously documenting it, and sending the
report --- only to be told that someone else beat you to it or that the
bug isn't critical enough. This can lead to frustration, self-doubt, and
eventually burnout.

### B. Uncertainty of Rewards {#2c82 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="2c82"}

One of the darkest realities of bug hunting is the unpredictability of
rewards. Even if you manage to discover a vulnerability, there's no
guarantee you'll be compensated fairly --- or at all. Companies may
decide to downplay the severity of a bug, lowering the bounty. In some
cases, they may even ignore your submission altogether. Many platforms
allow the companies to decide the bounty, which often means bug hunters
are at their mercy.

### 2. The Competitive Nature of Bug Bounties {#d9d1 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="d9d1"}

Bug hunting platforms often resemble a gladiatorial arena, where
thousands of skilled hunters are competing for the same prize. The sheer
volume of participants can make it difficult to stand out, especially
when many are equally skilled or have access to better tools and
resources.

### A. Saturation {#9e1b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="9e1b"}

With platforms like HackerOne and Bugcrowd gaining widespread
popularity, more and more people are entering the bug bounty scene. This
increase in competition means that even if you find a vulnerability,
someone else might have already submitted the same one. As the field
becomes saturated, the chances of receiving a bounty decrease
significantly. Furthermore, bug hunting often becomes a race where the
first to submit wins, leading to fierce and sometimes toxic competition.

### B. The Rise of "Super Hackers" {#69a1 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="69a1"}

Another dark reality is the rise of "super hackers" who dominate bug
bounty platforms. These are individuals or teams who have significant
experience, advanced tools, and deep knowledge, allowing them to
consistently rank high on leaderboards. For newcomers or
less-experienced hunters, this can be incredibly discouraging. Competing
against these seasoned professionals creates an uneven playing field,
where your efforts may feel insignificant.

### 3. The Exploitation of Bug Hunters {#5df6 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="5df6"}

While many companies genuinely appreciate bug hunters, there are
instances where companies exploit the system, using bug hunters to
improve their security without compensating them fairly.

### A. Scope Limitations {#e4ba .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="e4ba"}

One tactic that some companies employ is limiting the scope of their
bounty programs. They may invite hunters to find bugs but restrict them
to certain areas of their platform. In practice, this often means that
the most critical bugs --- which could affect the entire
platform --- are out of scope, leaving only the smaller, less
significant vulnerabilities open for hunting. This severely limits the
potential rewards for hunters while allowing companies to address
critical security concerns for free.

### B. Misuse of "Won't Fix" {#7c09 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="7c09"}

Another way companies exploit hunters is by using the "won't fix" tag.
Even after you submit a valid bug report, the company can decide not to
address the vulnerability, claiming that it doesn't align with their
security priorities or that it's too costly to fix. This leaves the
hunter with no compensation, despite having invested time and effort
into identifying the bug.

### C. Unpaid Bug Reports {#d521 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="d521"}

Some companies have also been accused of using bug bounty programs to
gather free vulnerability reports without any intention of paying
hunters. In these cases, companies may accept the report, fix the bug,
but provide no compensation. This unethical practice is one of the worst
forms of exploitation and is unfortunately a reality in the bug hunting
community.

### 4. The Psychological Toll {#6117 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6117"}

Bug hunting is mentally taxing. The constant pressure to find bugs, the
rejection of reports, and the uncertainty surrounding payouts can take a
serious toll on a hunter's mental health.

### A. Isolation {#c854 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="c854"}

Many bug hunters work in isolation, often spending long hours in front
of their computers, pouring over lines of code or testing various attack
vectors. This isolation can lead to feelings of loneliness, especially
when success is elusive. The solitary nature of bug hunting makes it
difficult to connect with others, and when compounded with the
challenges of the field, it can lead to mental health issues like
anxiety and depression.

### B. Burnout {#a280 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="a280"}

The constant cycle of working late hours, researching, testing, and
submitting reports, only to face rejection or minimal rewards, can lead
to burnout. This is especially common in the bug hunting community,
where the work is often unpaid or underpaid, and the competition is
fierce. The emotional roller coaster of excitement followed by
disappointment becomes draining over time.

### 5. Legal Risks and Ethical Dilemmas {#79b4 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="79b4"}

Bug hunting exists in a legal gray area. While bug bounty programs
provide legal protection for hunters, there are still risks involved,
especially when engaging in "out-of-scope" hacking activities or testing
systems that don't explicitly allow it.

### A. Overstepping Boundaries {#b1a3 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b1a3"}

One of the constant risks bug hunters face is accidentally overstepping
the boundaries of a bug bounty program. Sometimes, in their enthusiasm
to find vulnerabilities, hunters may test areas that are out of scope,
unintentionally breaking the law. This can lead to legal consequences,
including lawsuits, fines, or even criminal charges.

### B. Ethical Dilemmas {#48d9 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="48d9"}

There's also the ethical dilemma of how far a hunter should go when
testing a vulnerability. Some vulnerabilities require deep exploitation
to prove their validity, but doing so might cause damage to a company's
systems or data. Hunters are often faced with the challenge of proving
the bug without crossing ethical boundaries. This puts bug hunters in a
precarious situation where they must navigate both technical and moral
complexities.

### 6. The Financial Reality {#6e97 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6e97"}

Contrary to popular belief, bug hunting is not a guaranteed source of
income. While some hunters have made significant amounts of money, most
do not earn a livable wage from it.

### A. Inconsistent Income {#48c7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="48c7"}

Bug hunting provides an inconsistent income stream. Even the best
hunters may experience long dry spells where they find no bugs or their
submissions go unrewarded. For most hunters, bug hunting is not a
reliable full-time job but a side hustle or hobby that occasionally pays
off. Depending on it for a steady income can lead to financial strain.

### B. The Cost of Tools and Training {#8448 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="8448"}

Successful bug hunters invest in expensive tools, training, and
resources to stay competitive. From advanced vulnerability scanners to
specialized training courses, the costs can add up quickly. This makes
it difficult for beginners, who may not have the financial means to
invest in these resources, to compete with more experienced hunters who
have access to better tools.

### 7. The Psychological Aspect of Bug Submission {#8647 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="8647"}

Bug hunters need to have thick skin and a resilient mindset to cope with
the ups and downs.

### A. Harsh Criticism of Bug Reports {#90c6 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="90c6"}

After submitting a bug, you may face criticism from the company's
security team, reviewers, or peers. Many hunters face harsh feedback,
sometimes unwarranted, on their submitted reports. These critiques may
focus on the method used, the severity assigned to the bug, or the
clarity of the report itself. Over time, this feedback can affect one's
confidence and motivation.

### B. The Frustration of 'Duplicate' Bugs {#63c7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="63c7"}

Nothing is more disheartening than spending hours or even days hunting a
bug, documenting the process in detail, only to submit it and discover
it has already been reported. The growing number of hunters means that
duplication of efforts is common, and this results in no reward for the
second hunter, despite their efforts.

### 8. Is Bug Hunting Still Worth It? {#8b54 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="8b54"}

Despite the challenges, bug hunting remains a rewarding endeavor for
those passionate about cybersecurity. The knowledge gained from hunting
bugs is invaluable, and the thrill of finding a critical vulnerability
can be addictive. Moreover, bug bounty platforms continue to evolve,
offering better rewards, more transparency, and broader scopes to
incentivize hunters.

However, bug hunting is not the goldmine that many people assume it to
be. It requires perseverance, mental toughness, and a willingness to
face the darker realities of the profession. For those considering
entering the field, it's crucial to manage expectations, understand the
risks, and be prepared for the psychological and financial challenges
that come with it.

### Final Thoughts {#2535 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="2535"}

Bug hunting is a double-edged sword. While it offers the excitement of
the chase and the potential for significant financial rewards, it also
comes with its fair share of challenges and downsides. Hunters need to
be aware of the emotional and financial toll the field can take, as well
as the competitive and sometimes exploitative nature of bug bounty
platforms.

In the end, bug hunting is not just about finding vulnerabilities; it's
about resilience, persistence, and navigating the complex, and often
darker, aspects of cybersecurity. Those who succeed are not just skilled
hackers but also have the mental fortitude to endure the challenges and
setbacks that come with the territory.
