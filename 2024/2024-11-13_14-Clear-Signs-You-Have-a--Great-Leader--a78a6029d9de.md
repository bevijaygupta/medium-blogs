### **14 Clear Signs You Have a "Great Leader"** {#f0a6 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading .graf--title name="f0a6"}

In any work environment, the impact of a true leader goes far beyond the
title they hold. Great leaders have an intangible quality that makes the
workspace feel dynamic and supportive. They inspire growth, instill
confidence, and prioritize the development of their team over personal
gains. Here, I'll share 14 definitive signs of a great leader,
illustrated by some of the most touching messages I've received from my
closest collaborators. If you recognize these traits in someone you work
with, you're indeed lucky to have a leader who values you and your
growth.

### 1. They Communicate with Transparency {#8f5c .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="8f5c"}

A great leader understands the importance of clear, honest
communication. They don't shy away from difficult conversations and
value open dialogue with their team. When leaders communicate
transparently, they create a culture of trust and inclusivity, making
everyone feel informed and valued. Transparency also helps the team
understand the rationale behind decisions, which can be especially
reassuring during challenging times.

*One of my team members once texted me, "I always know where I stand
with you. Thanks for always being open." This transparency made them
feel secure in their roles and motivated to give their best.*

### 2. They Criticize Privately and Thoroughly {#b41d .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b41d"}

Constructive criticism is essential for growth, but a great leader knows
that delivering it with sensitivity is key. They offer feedback in a
private setting to avoid any embarrassment, ensuring that the
conversation is focused on improvement rather than judgment. They invest
time in explaining the areas where improvement is needed and providing
specific examples.

*I once received a heartfelt message saying, "I appreciate that you
always give feedback in private; it helps me focus on improving without
feeling disheartened." This approach allows employees to learn without
feeling discouraged.*

### 3. They Take Intelligent Risks {#84c7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="84c7"}

True leaders understand that innovation often involves taking risks.
Rather than playing it safe, they push boundaries to foster creativity
and progress. These leaders are strategic risk-takers who calculate
potential outcomes and accept the possibility of failure as part of the
journey. By taking intelligent risks, they inspire their team to embrace
challenges and think outside the box.

*An employee once told me, "Working with you makes me feel like I'm
allowed to try new things without the fear of failing." This atmosphere
of freedom enables individuals to discover their full potential.*

### 4. They Have a Knack for Attracting Talent {#2cc7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="2cc7"}

Great leaders attract talented individuals who resonate with their
vision. They have a magnetic energy that draws like-minded, driven
people toward them. Whether it's their charisma, dedication, or
expertise, these leaders naturally build a team of individuals who are
passionate about contributing to the organization's success.

*One colleague mentioned, "You have this way of bringing in the right
people. I love working in a team where everyone feels like they belong."
This natural ability to attract talent builds a culture of excellence.*

### 5. Emotional Intelligence Is Their Sixth Sense {#797f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="797f"}

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is one of the most crucial qualities of
effective leadership. Leaders with high EQ understand their own emotions
and can empathize with others. They can sense when someone on the team
is struggling and provide the necessary support without being intrusive.
They approach situations calmly, knowing that their reactions set the
tone for the team.

*A team member once messaged, "You always seem to know when I'm stressed
before I even say anything." This sensitivity helps foster a workplace
where people feel understood and appreciated.*

### 6. They Focus on Systems {#3655 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="3655"}

A great leader focuses on systems that make work efficient and
consistent. Instead of relying solely on people's efforts, they design
processes that guide work smoothly. By implementing effective systems,
they create an organized, predictable environment where the team can
thrive and perform optimally.

*One of my team members appreciated this approach by saying, "The
systems you put in place make it easier to do my job well." This
attention to structure shows that they care about making work simpler
and more effective for everyone.*

### 7. They Actually Listen to You {#aeea .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="aeea"}

One of the most empowering aspects of having a great leader is knowing
that they genuinely listen to you. When leaders actively listen, they
show respect and foster a culture where everyone feels valued. A great
leader listens to understand, not just to respond, and ensures that each
voice on their team is heard.

*I once received a message, "Thank you for always listening to my ideas;
it makes me feel like I'm truly part of the team." Listening empowers
employees and strengthens the bond between leaders and their teams.*

### 8. Adaptability Is Their Competitive Advantage {#3a18 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="3a18"}

In a fast-paced world, adaptability is a key trait of successful
leaders. Great leaders remain flexible and adjust their strategies when
needed. They encourage their teams to be adaptable, demonstrating that
change is an opportunity for growth rather than an obstacle.

*An employee texted, "I love how you're always open to new ways of doing
things." This adaptability creates a culture of resilience and
flexibility, essential for any team facing ever-evolving challenges.*

### 9. They Embrace Feedback {#6514 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6514"}

Feedback is essential for any leader who seeks growth. Great leaders
actively seek input from their team to understand how they can improve.
By embracing feedback, they demonstrate humility and a commitment to
continuous learning, setting a powerful example for their team.

*One employee shared, "It means a lot that you actually ask for our
feedback." By valuing feedback, a leader fosters a team that feels
empowered to contribute ideas and suggestions.*

### 10. They Give Credit Publicly {#d2d0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="d2d0"}

Recognizing achievements boosts morale and motivates people to maintain
high performance. Great leaders ensure that credit is given where it's
due, especially in public settings. Celebrating achievements openly
creates a positive environment where individuals feel valued for their
contributions.

*I received a heartfelt text saying, "Thanks for always acknowledging
our work publicly; it means a lot." Giving credit publicly reinforces a
culture of respect and appreciation.*

### 11. They Adopt a "Relationship Fast" Attitude {#41d1 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="41d1"}

While relationships are vital, great leaders know that boundaries are
equally important. They maintain a professional distance that allows
them to lead without personal bias. By adopting a "relationship fast"
attitude, they balance closeness with respect for boundaries, fostering
a productive and unbiased work environment.

*One of my colleagues messaged, "I appreciate how you manage to keep it
professional but still supportive." This balance ensures that the
leader's relationship with the team remains objective and fair.*

### 12. They Develop Other Leaders {#7ed2 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="7ed2"}

Great leaders see leadership as a legacy, not a monopoly. They focus on
building other leaders within their team, encouraging individuals to
take initiative and grow. This development of future leaders creates a
ripple effect of positive leadership throughout the organization.

*An employee once told me, "You've taught me how to lead others."
Developing leaders empowers individuals and strengthens the organization
as a whole.*

### 13. They Don't Fear Giving Out Their Secrets {#44c7 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="44c7"}

A true leader is confident enough to share their insights, tools, and
strategies with their team. Rather than holding back knowledge for
personal gain, they empower others by openly sharing valuable
information. This transparency and willingness to help others grow
creates a collaborative environment where everyone feels supported.

*One team member shared, "Thanks for not keeping your strategies to
yourself; it's helped me so much." This generosity shows that a great
leader prioritizes team success over personal gain.*

### 14. They Have an Informal yet "Let's Get to Work" Aura {#e598 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="e598"}

Great leaders create a relaxed yet productive atmosphere. They don't
need to rely on formalities to earn respect, as their natural authority
and commitment inspire people to work hard. This balance between
friendliness and professionalism allows the team to feel comfortable yet
focused on achieving their goals.

*One employee messaged, "You have this amazing way of making work feel
less like work." This quality enables a leader to create a supportive
and driven team environment.*

### Conclusion: So, Did You Get Lucky? {#85ee .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="85ee"}

If your leader exemplifies even half of these qualities, you're indeed
fortunate. These 14 signs distinguish great leaders who understand the
balance between driving results and fostering a nurturing environment.
They don't just lead --- they inspire, they support, and they elevate
everyone around them.
