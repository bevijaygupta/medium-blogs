### Understanding Scammers' Tactics {#2423 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="2423"}

Scammers often rely on psychological manipulation, exploiting fear,
greed, or urgency to make their schemes effective. They employ
techniques such as phishing, vishing (voice phishing), and smishing (SMS
phishing) to steal personal and financial information. Let's break down
ten prevalent scam tactics to help you recognize them.

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Fake TRAI Claims of Illegal Activity Linked to Your¬†Number {#c622 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="c622"}

**How it works:**\
Scammers impersonate officials from the Telecom Regulatory Authority of
India (TRAI), claiming your phone number has been involved in illegal
activities. They may threaten to block your number or take legal action
unless you comply with their demands.

**Why it works:**\
The fear of losing access to your phone number and the legal
consequences can make victims act impulsively.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Never share your personal information or OTP over the phone.]{#cf2e}
- [Confirm such claims directly with TRAI via their official website or
  helpline.]{#95d0}
- [Be skeptical of unsolicited calls from individuals claiming to be
  government officials.]{#785f}

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ "Parcel Stuck at Customs" Demanding Payments {#7c99 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="7c99"}

**How it works:**\
You receive a call or email claiming a parcel in your name is stuck at
customs. The scammer demands payment for clearance, taxes, or duties.

**Why it works:**\
The thought of losing a valuable item creates a sense of urgency and
compliance.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Verify parcel deliveries with the courier or logistics
  company.]{#4173}
- [Never pay fees or share personal information without
  verification.]{#2c1c}
- [Be cautious of unsolicited tracking links.]{#d8b7}

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ Threats of "Digital Arrest" or Online Interrogation {#704c .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="704c"}

**How it works:**\
Scammers threaten victims with immediate digital arrest for alleged
cybercrimes unless they cooperate. They may demand fines or personal
information.

**Why it works:**\
Fear of legal trouble and the intimidation of "official-sounding" terms
push people into compliance.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Know that no legitimate authority will arrest you digitally.]{#81a5}
- [Report such calls to your local cybercrime cell.]{#9142}
- [Avoid engaging with the caller.]{#538e}

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ False Claims About Family Members Being¬†Arrested {#c260 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="c260"}

**How it works:**\
The scammer pretends to be a police officer or lawyer, claiming a family
member is under arrest. They demand money for bail or legal fees.

**Why it works:**\
Emotional manipulation through concern for loved ones makes victims act
without verifying the information.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Call the family member directly to confirm their safety.]{#7077}
- [Verify claims with the local police station.]{#8027}
- [Avoid transferring money without solid proof.]{#fe10}

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ Get-Rich-Quick Trading¬†Schemes {#9846 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="9846"}

**How it works:**\
Scammers promise significant returns on investments in forex,
cryptocurrency, or stock trading. They often present fake success
stories and testimonials.

**Why it works:**\
The lure of easy money and the pressure to act fast tricks people into
investing.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Research thoroughly before investing in any trading scheme.]{#9de5}
- [Avoid schemes promising guaranteed high returns.]{#a19d}
- [Consult a certified financial advisor.]{#050c}

### 7Ô∏è‚É£ Fake Credit Card Fraud Confirmations {#aef0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="aef0"}

**How it works:**\
You receive a call or SMS claiming suspicious activity on your credit
card. The scammer asks for card details to "secure" your account.

**Why it works:**\
Fear of financial loss leads victims to act without verifying the
information.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Contact your bank directly using official helpline numbers.]{#2296}
- [Never share card details, PINs, or OTPs over the phone.]{#1d56}
- [Block and report suspicious numbers.]{#3ab9}

### 8Ô∏è‚É£ Mistaken Money Transfers Asking for¬†Refunds {#11a9 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="11a9"}

**How it works:**\
Scammers claim they mistakenly transferred money to your account and
request a refund. They might use fake screenshots as proof.

**Why it works:**\
People's desire to do the right thing can lead them to fall for this
trick.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Check your account for actual deposits before taking action.]{#46d0}
- [Report such incidents to your bank.]{#fc6d}
- [Avoid refunding money unless confirmed by your bank.]{#476b}

### 9Ô∏è‚É£ Fake KYC Updates Through¬†Links {#e6ea .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="e6ea"}

**How it works:**\
Scammers send SMS or emails with links to "update your KYC." Clicking
the link leads to a fake website designed to steal your information.

**Why it works:**\
Banks often emphasize KYC updates, making this scam appear credible.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Always update KYC information directly at the bank or through
  official apps.]{#491b}
- [Do not click on unsolicited links.]{#97fa}
- [Verify communication with your bank before taking any action.]{#7b26}

### üîü False Tax Refund Notifications {#f8b2 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="f8b2"}

**How it works:**\
You receive an email or SMS claiming you are eligible for a tax refund.
The message contains a link to submit your bank details.

**Why it works:**\
The prospect of getting money back from taxes can lead victims to act
impulsively.

**How to stay safe:**

- [Access your tax information only through official government
  portals.]{#8a89}
- [Avoid sharing financial details via email or SMS.]{#996d}
- [Report phishing emails to your country's tax department.]{#8854}

### üí° Key Safety Tips to Protect¬†Yourself {#c6ea .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="c6ea"}

Here are some general practices to safeguard against scams:

### 1. Always Verify Information {#b5f0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b5f0"}

- [Cross-check claims with official sources.]{#e70d}
- [Contact customer care or the appropriate authority directly.]{#0796}

### 2. Never Click Suspicious Links {#6c5f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="6c5f"}

- [Hover over links to check their actual destination.]{#b51e}
- [Use antivirus software to block malicious links.]{#c0a3}

### 3. Confirm with Banks Before¬†Acting {#915f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="915f"}

- [Reach out to your bank using official contact methods.]{#9e18}
- [Avoid sharing financial information with anyone claiming to represent
  your bank.]{#3229}

### 4. Avoid Sharing Personal¬†Details {#077b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="077b"}

- [Be cautious about what you share online or over the phone.]{#53f2}
- [Use multi-factor authentication for added security.]{#0882}

### 5. Report Suspicious Calls Immediately {#b785 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="b785"}

- [Save and block scammer numbers.]{#10f2}
- [Report incidents to cybercrime cells or helplines.]{#b754}
