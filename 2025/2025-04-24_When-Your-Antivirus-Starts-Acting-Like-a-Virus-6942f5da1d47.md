### 1. Use Windows Defender (Yes,Â Really) {#786d .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="786d"}

If you're on Windows 10 or 11, Microsoft Defender has matured into a
surprisingly competent, lightweight option. It doesn't scream at you for
using Notepad, and it doesn't sell your browsing habits to the cyber
mafia.

### 2. Complement with CommonÂ Sense {#9836 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="9836"}

- [Trust your gut: if a site looks shady, it probably is.]{#f725}

### 3. Use Malwarebytes or ESET for ProÂ Users {#5a6b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="5a6b"}

### Final Thoughts: Antivirus orÂ Frenemy? {#5ba0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="5ba0"}
