### When There Is No Guarantee of Success, Is It Still Justified to Run After It? {#b479 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading .graf--title name="b479"}

<figure id="8ce4" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--h3">
<img

No roadmap.\
No certainty.\
No promise that it will work.

Just an idea, a feeling, and a question that keeps showing up in your
head:

**"Is it even worth trying?"**

This question is not philosophical anymore.\
It is practical.\
It is emotional.\
It is deeply personal.

Gen Z asks it before choosing a career.\
[Founder](https://vijaykumargupta.in/){.markup--anchor .markup--p-anchor
data-href="https://vijaykumargupta.in/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"}s
ask it before quitting stable jobs.\
Creators ask it before hitting "publish."\
Professionals ask it before switching industries.\
Students ask it before choosing passion over safety.

And the honest answer?

There is no guarantee.\
Not now.\
Not ever.

So... **should you still chase it?**

### The Lie We Grew Up Believing {#2db5 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="2db5"}

Most of us were raised on a silent promise:

"If you work hard, you will succeed."

But real life didn't sign that contract.

You worked hard.\
You studied.\
You sacrificed weekends.\
You stayed disciplined.

And still:

- [Opportunities went to someone else]{#78fe}
- [Businesses failed]{#0b98}
- [Content didn't take off]{#ed28}
- [Interviews didn't convert]{#f0cd}
- [Effort didn't equal outcome]{#5027}

That is when the confusion begins.

**"What's the point of running after something that may never work?"**

This is where many people stop.

Not because they are lazy.\
But because they are tired of hoping.

### Gen Z Is Not Lazy --- They're Risk-Aware {#7139 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="7139"}

Let's clear something first.

Gen Z is not scared of hard work.\
They are scared of **wasted effort**.

They saw:

- [Parents burn out]{#2165}
- [Talented people underpaid]{#a5f9}
- [Degrees lose value]{#1a22}
- [Loyal employees replaced]{#81ff}
- [Stability collapse overnight]{#5448}

So when Gen Z asks,\
"Is there a guarantee?"

They're not weak.\
They're realistic.

They don't want blind hustle.\
They want **meaningful risk**.

But here's the paradox:

**The very things worth pursuing rarely come with guarantees.**

### Success Has Never Been Guaranteed --- We Just Pretended It Was {#0a95 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="0a95"}

Let's zoom out.

Was there ever a guarantee?

- [No guarantee that education leads to employment]{#f8f4}
- [No guarantee that loyalty leads to growth]{#0e38}
- [No guarantee that business leads to profit]{#e9f7}
- [No guarantee that talent leads to recognition]{#6aca}
- [No guarantee that effort leads to reward]{#0c68}

The "guarantee" was always an illusion created by:

- [Survivorship bias]{#2a7f}
- [Social conditioning]{#4ec1}
- [Highlight reels]{#55b2}
- [Old economic models]{#25ac}

The truth?

**Uncertainty is not new. We are just more aware of it now.**

### Why People Still Chase Uncertain Paths {#ee63 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="ee63"}

Despite knowing all this, people still try.

They still build startups.\
They still create content.\
They still switch careers.\
They still start over at 30, 40, even 50.

Why?

Because some things hurt more than failure.

### The Pain of Regret Is Heavier Than the Pain of Trying {#147f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="147f"}

Failure hurts, yes.

But regret stays.

Failure says:\
"I tried, it didn't work."

Regret says:\
"I never found out."

One fades with time.\
The other grows louder.

Most people don't regret the risks they took.\
They regret the ones they didn't.

That conversation you didn't start.\
That idea you didn't launch.\
That skill you didn't pursue.\
That version of you that never got a chance.

### Founders Know This Better Than Anyone {#d152 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="d152"}

Ask any founder.

They didn't start because success was guaranteed.\
They started because **staying still felt worse**.

Founders choose uncertainty not because they love chaos,\
but because they value **control over outcome**.

Even failure taught them:

- [Skills]{#fee3}
- [Decision-making]{#366e}
- [Pattern recognition]{#ca9c}
- [Emotional resilience]{#c977}
- [[Market
  understanding](https://einitial24.com/digital-marketing-course/){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor
  data-href="https://einitial24.com/digital-marketing-course/"
  rel="noopener" target="_blank"}]{#941f}

A failed startup still upgrades the founder.

A safe path that suffocates you?\
That upgrades nothing.

### Running After Something Is Not About Winning {#76ee .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="76ee"}

This is where most people misunderstand success.

Running after something does not mean:\
"I must win."

It means:\
"I must explore."

You are not chasing certainty.\
You are chasing **alignment**.

Alignment between:

- [What you care about]{#aa63}
- [What you are good at]{#769e}
- [What the world needs]{#87a5}
- [What you are willing to struggle for]{#2543}

Success is not the destination.\
Growth is.

### What People Don't Tell You About 'Safe' Choices {#6e19 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="6e19"}

Safe choices are not risk-free.

They just delay risk.

- [A job can disappear]{#9465}
- [An industry can die]{#cd4a}
- [A degree can become irrelevant]{#a7f1}
- [A company can downsize]{#70ea}
- [A skill can expire]{#1267}

The difference?

You didn't choose that risk.\
It happened to you.

Chasing uncertainty at least gives you ownership.

### Trying Is a Skill --- Not a Gamble {#b9dd .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b9dd"}

People think trying is random.

It isn't.

Trying teaches you:

- [How to adapt]{#6543}
- [How to recover]{#865b}
- [How to think independently]{#725a}
- [How to build leverage]{#265a}
- [How to trust yourself]{#7e65}

Every attempt compounds internally.

Even when externally it looks like nothing happened.

### Gen Z's Advantage: Low Attachment, High Awareness {#5f3a .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="5f3a"}

Gen Z understands something earlier generations learned late:

You don't need to attach your identity to outcomes.

You can:

- [Try without being defined by failure]{#2a11}
- [Pivot without shame]{#c8c1}
- [Restart without apology]{#be48}
- [Learn without validation]{#20df}

This flexibility is power.

The world rewards adaptability now.\
Not certainty.

### The Question Is Not 'Will I Succeed?' {#b50f .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="b50f"}

That question will paralyze you.

Ask better questions:

- ["What will I learn if this fails?"]{#9bec}
- ["Who will I become if I try?"]{#eb97}
- ["Can I live with not knowing?"]{#ba42}
- ["What kind of struggle am I choosing?"]{#e348}

Every path has pain.

Choose your pain intentionally.

### Most Breakthroughs Look Like Bad Ideas at First {#4715 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="4715"}

Every major shift was once uncertain.

- [Freelancing was risky]{#8758}
- [Content creation was childish]{#d089}
- [Startups were unstable]{#d4a6}
- [Remote work was impossible]{#26e6}
- [AI was a joke]{#4543}
- [Internet careers were not real jobs]{#9e8a}

Now?\
They define the economy.

Uncertainty is not a red flag.\
It is often a signal.

### Running After Something Changes You Even If You Lose {#e2bb .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="e2bb"}

You gain:

- [Confidence in decision-making]{#63a3}
- [Respect for yourself]{#0eda}
- [Proof of courage]{#7a4e}
- [Emotional maturity]{#a17c}
- [Self-trust]{#34d8}

These things don't show up on resumes.\
But they show up in life.

### The Real Cost Is Not Failure --- It's Suppression {#ff18 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="ff18"}

What happens if you don't try?

- [You shrink your ambitions]{#5909}
- [You rationalize unhappiness]{#3f4b}
- [You normalize dissatisfaction]{#0804}
- [You live on autopilot]{#69e8}
- [You slowly disconnect from yourself]{#d4fa}

That is not safety.\
That is silent erosion.

### Success Is Not Guaranteed --- Growth Is {#83f0 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="83f0"}

This is the most honest deal life offers:

No guarantee of success.\
But a guarantee of growth if you engage fully.

And growth compounds.

The version of you who tries\
is always stronger than the version who waits.

### So... Is It Justified to Run After It? {#9204 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="9204"}

Yes.

Not because success is guaranteed.

But because:

- [Meaning is built, not promised]{#2094}
- [Identity is shaped by action]{#e328}
- [Confidence comes from movement]{#99e9}
- [Self-respect comes from courage]{#6f41}
- [Life rewards engagement, not certainty]{#1ce0}

You don't chase dreams because they are safe.\
You chase them because **they are yours**.

### Final Thought {#4750 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="4750"}

If there were a guarantee,\
everyone would do it.

The absence of certainty is not a reason to stop.\
It is the reason your effort matters.

Run after it.\
Not recklessly.\
But honestly.\
Intentionally.\
With awareness.

Even if it doesn't work out,\
**you will.**

And sometimes,\
that is the real success.
