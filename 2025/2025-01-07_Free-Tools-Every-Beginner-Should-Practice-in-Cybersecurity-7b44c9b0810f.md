### 1. VirusTotal {#bd70 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--p name="bd70"}

- [**Website**: [VirusTotal](https://www.virustotal.com){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://www.virustotal.com"
  rel="noopener" target="_blank"}]{#f466}
- [**Purpose**: Analyzing files and URLs for potential threats.]{#5e22}

VirusTotal is a powerful online platform that aggregates data from
multiple antivirus engines, URL scanners, and other tools to detect
viruses, worms, trojans, and other forms of malware. Beginners can
upload files or paste URLs to scan them for malicious content. The tool
provides insights into malware behavior and is invaluable for
understanding threat patterns.

#### How to Use: {#1a6c .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="1a6c"}

1.  [Visit the VirusTotal website.]{#82d8}
2.  [Upload a file or enter a URL for analysis.]{#c7a8}
3.  [Review the detailed report generated, which includes threat
    intelligence from various sources.]{#9820}

### 2. CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) {#2782 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="2782"}

- [**Website**: [CVE](https://cve.mitre.org){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://cve.mitre.org" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#2cfb}
- [**Purpose**: Identifying known vulnerabilities and threats.]{#2651}

CVE is a globally recognized database that documents publicly disclosed
cybersecurity vulnerabilities. Each entry includes a unique identifier,
description, and references to help professionals and beginners
understand the nature of the vulnerability.

#### How to Use: {#c385 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="c385"}

1.  [Search the CVE database for vulnerabilities in software or systems
    you are studying.]{#7a15}
2.  [Review the CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System) score to
    assess the severity.]{#a1db}
3.  [Learn how attackers exploit these vulnerabilities and the
    recommended mitigations.]{#696c}

### 3. WHOIS {#7dd8 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="7dd8"}

- [**Website**: [WHOIS](https://lnkd.in/eTnM7SRA){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://lnkd.in/eTnM7SRA" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#b6bc}
- [**Purpose**: Investigating suspicious domains and identifying
  ownership.]{#0712}

WHOIS is a protocol used to query databases containing information about
registered domain names. It is essential for identifying domain
ownership, contact information, and registration details, making it
useful for investigating phishing campaigns or suspicious websites.

#### How to Use: {#b2e9 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="b2e9"}

1.  [Enter the domain name into a WHOIS lookup tool.]{#4dd7}
2.  [Analyze the registration details, including the registrant's name,
    email, and registrar.]{#4c13}
3.  [Use this information to trace malicious domains or validate
    legitimate ones.]{#70f8}

### 4. Shodan {#13ed .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="13ed"}

- [**Website**: [Shodan](https://shodan.io){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://shodan.io" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#82b1}
- [**Purpose**: Discovering exposed internet-connected devices.]{#d851}

Shodan is often referred to as the "Google of IoT devices." It scans the
internet for connected devices and provides information about their
configurations, vulnerabilities, and accessibility.

#### How to Use: {#a866 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="a866"}

1.  [Create a free Shodan account for basic access.]{#47bf}
2.  [Use search queries to find devices like webcams, routers, or
    industrial control systems.]{#3df4}
3.  [Analyze the results to understand the risks posed by exposed
    devices.]{#4dcf}

### 5. AlienVault OTX {#cde9 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="cde9"}

- [**Website**: [AlienVault
  OTX](https://lnkd.in/evBpRDRq){.markup--anchor .markup--li-anchor
  data-href="https://lnkd.in/evBpRDRq" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#8463}
- [**Purpose**: Sharing and accessing threat intelligence.]{#69c8}

AlienVault's Open Threat Exchange (OTX) is a community-driven platform
where users can share and discover real-time threat intelligence. It is
a great resource for learning about new attack vectors and indicators of
compromise (IOCs).

#### How to Use: {#906a .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="906a"}

1.  [Sign up for a free account on OTX.]{#5f6b}
2.  [Subscribe to threat feeds or "pulses" relevant to your
    interests.]{#ac9c}
3.  [Study the shared IOCs and integrate them into your learning or
    tools like SIEMs.]{#b737}

### 6. Nmap {#0446 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="0446"}

- [**Website**: [Nmap](https://www.nmap.org){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://www.nmap.org" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#6c64}
- [**Purpose**: Network discovery and scanning.]{#0106}

Nmap (Network Mapper) is a free and open-source tool used for network
discovery, management, and security auditing. It allows users to
identify hosts, services, and operating systems on a network.

#### How to Use: {#30b6 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="30b6"}

1.  [Download and install Nmap on your system.]{#be14}
2.  [Use basic commands like `nmap <IP address>`{.markup--code
    .markup--li-code} to scan a host.]{#9922}
3.  [Experiment with advanced options to identify open ports and running
    services.]{#a295}

### 7. Censys {#7b90 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="7b90"}

- [**Website**: [Censys](https://censys.com/){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://censys.com/" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#1700}
- [**Purpose**: Monitoring and discovering vulnerable
  devices/services.]{#903a}

Censys is another internet-wide scanning tool like Shodan but focuses
more on monitoring and analyzing internet-connected devices for
vulnerabilities.

#### How to Use: {#8969 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="8969"}

1.  [Create a free Censys account.]{#e786}
2.  [Search for IP addresses, domains, or certificates.]{#e70b}
3.  [Analyze the results to identify misconfigurations or
    vulnerabilities.]{#dad1}

### 8. Exploit Database {#f03b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="f03b"}

- [**Website**: [Exploit
  Database](https://www.exploit-db.com){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://www.exploit-db.com"
  rel="noopener" target="_blank"}]{#c258}
- [**Purpose**: Finding exploits and vulnerabilities/CVEs.]{#750c}

Exploit Database is a comprehensive archive of exploits and
proof-of-concepts (PoCs) for known vulnerabilities. It's an essential
resource for penetration testing and understanding real-world attacks.

#### How to Use: {#6772 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="6772"}

1.  [Search for exploits based on software, version, or CVE ID.]{#e307}
2.  [Download PoCs to study their mechanics in a controlled
    environment.]{#08a6}
3.  [Use them responsibly to test your systems or labs.]{#2d53}

### 9. DNSDumpster {#9905 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="9905"}

- [**Website**: [DNSDumpster](https://dnsdumpster.com/){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://dnsdumpster.com/" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#b085}
- [**Purpose**: Domain research and reconnaissance.]{#13f0}

DNSDumpster is a free domain research tool that helps map DNS records
and provides a visualization of a domain's infrastructure.

#### How to Use: {#8e1a .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="8e1a"}

1.  [Enter a domain name to perform a lookup.]{#9c4d}
2.  [Analyze the DNS records, including MX, TXT, and A records.]{#0e9f}
3.  [Use the data to understand the domain's structure and potential
    vulnerabilities.]{#72a8}

### 10. OSINT Framework {#9b05 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="9b05"}

- [**Website**: [OSINT
  Framework](https://lnkd.in/giwxHNaY){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://lnkd.in/giwxHNaY" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#e33b}
- [**Purpose**: A collection of free tools for open-source
  intelligence.]{#6b00}

The OSINT Framework is a curated directory of tools and resources for
gathering open-source intelligence. It covers various categories like
email tracing, social media analysis, and IP tracking.

#### How to Use: {#ca8d .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="ca8d"}

1.  [Browse the framework to find tools suited to your needs.]{#8393}
2.  [Experiment with tools like Maltego, Spiderfoot, or
    Sherlock.]{#92e2}
3.  [Apply them in scenarios like footprinting or recon.]{#626c}

### 11. Metasploit Framework (Community Edition) {#e21b .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="e21b"}

- [**Website**: [Metasploit
  Framework](https://www.metasploit.com){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://www.metasploit.com"
  rel="noopener" target="_blank"}]{#9029}
- [**Purpose**: Penetration testing and exploit development.]{#df79}

Metasploit is a widely-used framework for penetration testing. It allows
users to find, exploit, and validate vulnerabilities in systems.

#### How to Use: {#3330 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="3330"}

1.  [Download and install the community edition.]{#5e6e}
2.  [Use modules like `exploit`{.markup--code .markup--li-code} and
    `payload`{.markup--code .markup--li-code} to simulate attacks in a
    lab environment.]{#a28f}
3.  [Learn to use auxiliary modules for tasks like scanning and
    enumeration.]{#98fc}

### 12. Iplocation {#7e23 .graf .graf--h3 .graf-after--li name="7e23"}

- [**Website**: [Iplocation](https://lnkd.in/gaQpGjwY){.markup--anchor
  .markup--li-anchor data-href="https://lnkd.in/gaQpGjwY" rel="noopener"
  target="_blank"}]{#1386}
- [**Purpose**: Retrieving IP address information.]{#48ed}

Iplocation is a simple tool to find geolocation, ISP details, and other
metadata associated with an IP address. It's particularly useful for
tracing the source of suspicious network traffic.

#### How to Use: {#8fc7 .graf .graf--h4 .graf-after--p name="8fc7"}

1.  [Enter the IP address on the tool's website.]{#e8fe}
2.  [Review the results, including country, region, and ISP.]{#d7c4}
3.  [Cross-reference the data with other tools for deeper
    insights.]{#10d6}
