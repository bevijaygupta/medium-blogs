### Chapter 5: Advanced AI Hacking Tactics (aka "Tactical Faceplants") {#9579 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="9579"}

At some point, developers thought:\
¬†*"Maybe we should make AI smarter. Give it real-world penetration

Finally, it launched a "serious" attack:

- [Scanned the target network.]{#27bd}
- [Found **open ports**.]{#4b70}
- [Tried to "exploit" a printer.]{#43e1}

**Yes. A PRINTER.**

It sent 1000 exploit payloads to the printer.

The printer, being older and wiser, responded by:

- [Printing 500 pages of "**STOP HACKING ME YOU FOOL**."]{#95f7}
- [Going offline.]{#84be}
- [Filing for early retirement.]{#501d}

### Chapter 9: The Future of AI¬†Hacking {#ed01 .graf .graf--h3 .graf--leading name="ed01"}

Will AI eventually get better at hacking?

Maybe.

Will it ever replace a human hacker?

Probably... right after **clippy** from Microsoft Word becomes
President.

Until then, humans are safe.\
¬†Mostly because no human hacker would ever write phishing emails like:

> ***"We hacked your computer. Kindly reply with your favorite pizza
> topping.****"*

Or try to perform reconnaissance by scrolling through Pinterest boards
titled "**cybersecurity aesthetic**."

We're not saying AI can't hack.\
¬†We're just saying...

> ***Thank God it can't.***
